# Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy (GDMT) for Heart Failure

GDMT refers to the four-pillar drug therapy recommended by the ACC/AHA guidelines for patients with heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (HFrEF, EF ≤ 40%). All four drug classes have demonstrated mortality reduction in randomized controlled trials.

## The Four Pillars of GDMT

### 1. ACE Inhibitor / ARB / ARNI
- **First-line:** ARNI (sacubitril/valsartan, brand name Entresto) is preferred over ACEi/ARB
- **ACEi examples:** Enalapril 10-20mg BID, Lisinopril 20-40mg daily, Ramipril 5mg BID
- **ARB examples:** Valsartan 160mg BID, Losartan 50-150mg daily
- **ARNI:** Sacubitril/valsartan 97/103mg BID (target dose)
- ARNI reduced cardiovascular death and heart failure hospitalization by 20% compared to enalapril (PARADIGM-HF trial)
- Must wash out ACEi for 36 hours before starting ARNI to reduce angioedema risk

### 2. Beta-Blocker
- **Evidence-based beta-blockers only:** Carvedilol 25mg BID (50mg BID if >85kg), Metoprolol succinate 200mg daily, Bisoprolol 10mg daily
- Start low, titrate slowly (double dose every 2 weeks)
- Reduce heart rate, prevent remodeling, reduce sudden cardiac death
- Do NOT use in acute decompensated heart failure

### 3. Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonist (MRA)
- **Spironolactone** 25-50mg daily or **Eplerenone** 25-50mg daily
- Indicated for NYHA Class II-IV with EF ≤ 35%
- Monitor potassium closely — contraindicated if K+ > 5.0 mEq/L
- 30% reduction in mortality (RALES trial)
- Avoid if eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73m²

### 4. SGLT2 Inhibitor
- **Dapagliflozin** 10mg daily or **Empagliflozin** 10mg daily
- Newest addition to GDMT (added based on DAPA-HF and EMPEROR-Reduced trials)
- Benefits apply regardless of diabetes status
- 26% reduction in cardiovascular death and worsening heart failure
- Also provides renal protection
- Can be initiated at any point — does not require dose titration

## Treatment Escalation Approach
1. Start all four drug classes simultaneously when possible (rather than sequential)
2. Titrate to target doses over weeks to months
3. If EF remains ≤ 35% after 3 months of optimal GDMT, consider ICD for primary prevention
4. If QRS ≥ 150ms with LBBB, consider CRT (cardiac resynchronization therapy)
5. For persistent symptoms despite optimal GDMT, consider advanced therapies: LVAD or transplant

## For HFpEF (EF ≥ 50%)
- SGLT2 inhibitors are the only drug class with proven benefit (EMPEROR-Preserved trial)
- Diuretics for volume management
- Treat underlying conditions (hypertension, atrial fibrillation, obesity, coronary artery disease)
